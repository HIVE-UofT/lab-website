Guan has interdisciplinary training in medicine and health economics, which has shaped her interest in using data and innovation on healthcare research. With compassion in her heart and the mindset of an economist focused on maximizing utility under real-world constraints, she is particularly interested in applying artificial intelligence and machine learning to health data to support disease prevention, personalized care, and health equity. Her research focuses on how AI-driven approaches can help identify risk, inform decision-making, and improve care for vulnerable populations. She believes that technological innovation in healthcare should not only advance efficiency, but also ensure that its benefits reach the people who need them most.
